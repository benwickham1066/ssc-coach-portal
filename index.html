In-Call Reference

Quick Reference

Keep this open during calls. The methodology, the patterns, the ease questions — everything you need without hunting for it.

The Methodology Arc
Surface
Make the pattern visible. Not as a diagnosis — as a recognition. The client needs to see it themselves, in their own words, before anything else can happen.
Decode
Understand what the pattern was protecting. It made sense once — in a context that no longer exists. The work is to find that original logic and honour it.
Resolve
Give the client a genuine choice. Not willpower — mechanism. The pattern becomes optional. They can run it consciously or not at all. That's the shift.
The Seven Defensive Patterns
PatternWhat it looks likeWhat it stops
SmoothingAgrees, softens, says yes when they mean no. Keeps the peace at the cost of honesty.The honest disagreement that would have changed the outcome.
Deferring UpRefers decisions upward that they could and should make themselves.Speed, ownership, and the trust of the people they lead.
Going QuietSees the problem, says nothing. Withdraws when their voice is most needed.The early warning. By the time it surfaces, the cost has multiplied.
Shutting It DownCloses conversations before the real issue is resolved.The new idea. People stop bringing things because speaking up carries a cost.
DefendingProtects their position before the other person has finished talking.Feedback reaching them. Blind spots widen. The team stops trying.
Taking OverSteps in and solves what belongs to someone else.The team's ownership and growth. Capacity concentrates at the top.
FirefightingLives in urgency. Always reacting, rarely leading.The strategic conversation. The team runs in permanent crisis mode.
